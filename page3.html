How access to financial tools and technology shapes opportunity in Brazil

Introduction

Access to financial services and technology is one of the most powerful drivers of economic mobility. In Brazil, millions of people, particularly those in rural areas and low-income communities, have historically been excluded from formal banking, credit, and digital tools. Without access to these resources, families struggle to save, invest, or build long-term financial security.

This issue connects directly to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals because financial exclusion deepens inequality. When people cannot access credit or digital payment systems, they are shut out of the formal economy and left dependent on informal, often exploitative, alternatives.

Key Ideas

  • Millions of Brazilians lack access to formal banking and credit.
  • Technology like mobile banking and fintech is closing the gap rapidly.
  • Financial literacy is as important as access to financial tools.
  • Unequal tech access reinforces existing economic inequality.

The Financial Access Gap in Brazil

Brazil is one of the largest economies in the world, yet a significant portion of its population remains underbanked or unbanked. Low-income Brazilians are often unable to open bank accounts due to documentation requirements, fees, or lack of nearby branches. Without a bank account, receiving wages, saving money, or applying for loans becomes far more difficult.

The rise of fintech companies like Nubank has begun to change this. By offering no-fee digital accounts accessible via smartphone, these platforms have brought millions of previously excluded Brazilians into the formal financial system. However, smartphone ownership and reliable internet access are not universal, meaning the digital divide still limits who benefits.

Connection to the UN Goals

SDG 1: No Poverty

Access to financial tools gives low-income individuals the ability to save, invest, and build resilience against economic shocks.

SDG 9: Industry & Innovation

Expanding digital infrastructure and fintech innovation supports sustainable economic growth and broader participation in the economy.

SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

Financial inclusion directly reduces inequality by giving marginalized populations the same tools available to wealthier citizens.

Why It Matters

Financial and technological exclusion is not just an inconvenience. It is a structural barrier. When people cannot access credit, they cannot start businesses. When they cannot use digital payment systems, they are cut off from growing segments of the economy. For Brazil to reduce inequality, expanding access to financial services and technology must be treated as a public priority, not just a market opportunity.

Possible Solutions

  • Expand low-cost digital banking to underserved populations
  • Invest in rural broadband and smartphone access programs
  • Integrate financial literacy into school curricula
  • Support fintech regulation that protects consumers while encouraging innovation
  • Fund community technology centers in low-income neighborhoods
  • Partner NGOs with fintech companies to reach excluded communities

Conclusion

Technology and financial access are no longer luxuries. They are necessities for participating in the modern economy. Brazil has made meaningful progress through fintech innovation, but the benefits are not yet reaching everyone equally. By treating financial inclusion as a development priority and investing in the digital infrastructure needed to support it, Brazil can move closer to the equitable, sustainable future envisioned by the UN Sustainable Development Goals.
